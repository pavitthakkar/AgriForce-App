## 1. Getting Started

### Accessing AgriForce
AgriForce runs entirely in your web browser — no installation required.

1. Open your browser and navigate to your AgriForce app URL
2. You will land on the main dashboard
3. You will see four tabs at the top: **Builder**, **Analytics**, **Environment**, and **AI Assistant**

### Creating Your First Farm
When you first open AgriForce, you will be prompted to create a farm. Give your farm a name (e.g. "Tower Farm", "Rooftop Garden") and click **Create**.

## 2. Building Your Farm (Canvas Builder)

The **Builder** tab is where you design your vertical farm layout visually.

### Adding a Base
1. Click the **Builder** tab
2. Click **"Add Rack"**, **"Add Tower"**, or **"Add A-Frame"** and drag it anywhere on the canvas
3. Give your tower a name (e.g. "Tower 1", "First Rack")
4. Set the number of planting spots available on your base

### Assigning Crops to Spots
1. Click on the **"Add Plants"** accordion 
2. Type in a crop into the add (e.g. Lettuce, Kale, Basil, Tomatoes)
3. Click on an empty planting spot on your tower

### Adding Lighting Modules
1. Click on the **"Add Light"** accordion
2. Select your light type (LED, Metal Halide, High Pressure Sodium)
3. Assign it to a position on the tower

### Saving Your Layout
Click the **Save** button in the top right corner to save your farm layout. Your AI assistant will automatically have access to this layout data.

## 3. Plant Journal

The **Plant Journal** lets you log observations about your crops over time — tracking growth, health issues, and milestones.

### Adding a Journal Entry
1. Navigate to the **Builder** section
2. Click on a plant module
4. Enter your observation (e.g. "Leaves looking healthy, good color", "Slight yellowing on outer leaves"), the health status, and a photo.
5. The date is automatically recorded
6. Click **Save**

### Viewing Past Entries
All your journal entries are displayed chronologically. You can scroll through to track how your crops have changed over time.

### Tips for Good Journal Entries
- Be specific — "yellowing on lower leaves" is more useful than "plant looks bad"
- Log regularly — weekly entries give the AI better context
- Note any changes you made — "increased nutrient concentration today"

## 4. Environmental Controls

The **Environment** tab lets you log and monitor your farm's environmental conditions.

### Logging a Reading
1. Click the **Environment** tab
2. Enter your current readings:
   - **Temperature** (°C or °F)
   - **Humidity** (%)
   - **CO2 levels** (ppm)
   - **Light intensity**
   - and more
3. Click **Log Reading**

### Why This Matters
The more environmental data you log, the more specific your AI assistant's recommendations will be. For example, if your temperature is too high and your lettuce is showing stress, the AI can connect these two things and give you targeted advice.

### Recommended Ranges for Common Crops

| Crop | Temperature | Humidity | Light Hours |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lettuce | 18-22°C | 50-70% | 16 hrs |
| Basil | 20-25°C | 50-60% | 16-18 hrs |
| Kale | 15-22°C | 50-70% | 14-16 hrs |
| Tomatoes | 20-26°C | 60-80% | 18 hrs |
| Spinach | 15-20°C | 50-70% | 14-16 hrs |

## 5. AI Assistant

The **AI Assistant** tab is the heart of AgriForce — an intelligent farming assistant that knows your specific farm and can answer any farming question.

### Starting a Conversation
1. Click the **AI Assistant** tab
2. You will see a welcome message confirming the AI has access to your farm data
3. Type your question in the input box at the bottom
4. Press **Send**

### What You Can Ask
The AI assistant can help with a wide range of farming topics:

**Crop Health:**
- "Why are my lettuce leaves turning yellow?"
- "My basil looks droopy — what's wrong?"
- "How do I fix tip burn on my lettuce?"

**Growing Guidance:**
- "What are the best crops to grow in a vertical farm?"
- "How often should I water my tomatoes?"
- "When is my kale ready to harvest?"

**Nutrients & pH:**
- "What pH should my water be for lettuce?"
- "What does nitrogen deficiency look like?"
- "How do I fix iron deficiency in hydroponics?"

**Lighting:**
- "How many hours of light does basil need?"
- "What's the difference between LED and Metal Halide for growing?"
- "Is my current lighting setup good for leafy greens?"

**Environment:**
- "My temperature is 28°C — is that too hot for lettuce?"
- "What humidity level should I maintain for my crops?"
- "How do I reduce humidity in my grow space?"

**Farm Specific:**
- "What is planted in Tower 1?"
- "Based on my current setup, what should I plant next?"
- "How is my farm performing overall?"

### Getting the Best Responses
- **Be specific** — "my lettuce in Spot 3 is yellowing" gets a better answer than "my plant looks bad"
- **Include context** — mention recent changes you've made
- **Ask follow-up questions** — the AI remembers your conversation history

## 6. Image Analysis

AgriForce can analyze photos of your crops to identify diseases, pests, and health issues.

### How to Upload an Image
1. In the **AI Assistant** tab, click the 📷 **camera icon** next to the input box
2. Select a photo from your device
3. A preview of your image will appear above the input box
4. Click **"Analyze Image"**
5. Wait a few seconds for the AI to analyze your photo

### What the AI Looks For
- Plant and crop identification
- Health assessment (healthy, stressed, diseased)
- Visible diseases (powdery mildew, root rot, leaf spot)
- Pest damage (aphids, spider mites, fungus gnats)
- Nutrient deficiencies (yellowing, browning, spots)
- Specific recommendations based on your farm's current data

### Tips for Good Photos
- **Good lighting** — natural light or grow light, avoid flash
- **Close up** — get close enough to see leaf detail
- **Multiple angles** — photograph both top and underside of affected leaves
- **Focus on the problem area** — zoom in on yellowing, spots, or damage

### Important Notes
- AgriForce only analyzes plant and crop related images
- Images are processed in real-time and not stored permanently
- For best results, photograph the specific plant or leaf showing symptoms

## 7. Pinning & Managing Conversations

AgriForce lets you save important conversations for future reference.

### Pinning a Conversation
1. After a useful conversation, click the **Pin** button in the top right
2. Give your conversation a name
3. It will appear under **Pinned** conversations

### Accessing Pinned Conversations
Click **Pinned** in the top right to see all your saved conversations. Click any pinned conversation to review it.

### Starting a New Chat
Click **+ New Chat** to start a fresh conversation. Your previous conversations are saved if pinned.

## 8. Tips for Best Results

### Keep Your Farm Data Updated
The AI assistant is only as good as the data you give it. Regularly:
- ✅ Update your canvas when you plant or harvest crops
- ✅ Log environmental readings weekly
- ✅ Add journal entries when you notice changes

### Ask Specific Questions
| Instead of... | Ask... |
|---|---|
| "Why is my plant sick?" | "Why are the lower leaves of my kale in Spot 2 turning yellow?" |
| "What should I grow?" | "Based on my current lighting and temperature, what crops would grow best?" |
| "How do I fix this?" | "My lettuce has brown leaf edges — how do I fix tip burn?" |

### Use Image Analysis Regularly
Upload photos of your crops weekly — even healthy plants! This helps you:
- Catch problems early before they spread
- Track growth progress visually
- Get proactive recommendations

## 9. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Does the AI remember my previous conversations?**
A: The AI remembers your conversation within a single session. Pinned conversations can be reviewed but each new chat starts fresh. Your farm data (layout, environment, journal) is always available to the AI.

**Q: How accurate is the image analysis?**
A: The image analysis uses Llama 4 Scout, a state-of-the-art multimodal AI model. It is highly accurate for common crop diseases and health issues. However, always use it as a guide alongside your own observations — for serious issues, consult an agricultural expert.

**Q: Can I use AgriForce for soil-based growing?**
A: AgriForce is optimized for vertical and hydroponic farming but can provide general growing advice for soil-based systems as well.

**Q: Is my farm data private?**
A: Yes. Your farm data is stored in your personal account and is never shared with other users. See our [Privacy Policy](PRIVACY.md) for full details.

**Q: What crops does AgriForce support?**
A: AgriForce's AI is trained on data for hundreds of crops. It works best with common vertical farming crops like lettuce, basil, kale, spinach, tomatoes, strawberries, and microgreens, but can provide guidance on many other crops as well.

**Q: What if the AI gives me wrong advice?**
A: The AI is a guidance tool, not a replacement for expert advice. If you are unsure, cross-reference with a trusted agricultural source or consult an expert. You can also report issues via our [GitHub Issues page](https://github.com/YOUR_USERNAME/agriforce-rag-system/issues).
